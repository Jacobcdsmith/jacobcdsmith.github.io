## 8.1 Path-Integral Formulation (Rigorous Foundation)

### 8.1.1 Quantum Trajectories as Paths

**Definition 8.1.1** (Continuous Weak Measurement): Consider a quantum system evolving under control Hamiltonian \(H_C(\boldsymbol{\phi}(t))\) with weak continuous measurement:

$$d\mathcal{M} = \mathcal{A} dt + d\mathcal{W}(t)$$

Where:
- \(\mathcal{A}\): Measurement operator (weak coupling)
- \(d\mathcal{W}\): Wiener noise (quantum vacuum fluctuations)

The **quantum trajectory** \(\psi(t)\) evolves according to **stochastic Schrödinger equation**:

$$d|\psi\rangle = -\frac{i}{\hbar}H_C|\psi\rangle dt + \left(\sqrt{\gamma}\mathcal{A} - \frac{\gamma}{2}\mathcal{A}^\dagger\mathcal{A}\right)|\psi\rangle dt + \sqrt{\gamma}\mathcal{A}|\psi\rangle dW$$

Where \(\gamma\) is measurement strength.

### 8.1.2 Ensemble of Trajectories

**Definition 8.1.2** (Multicomputation Ensemble): The set of all possible trajectories generated by independent realizations of the stochastic evolution:

$$\{\psi_i(t) : i = 1, ..., N_{traj}\}$$

Each trajectory \(\psi_i\) represents one **computational path**.

The ensemble **average** recovers the Lindblad master equation:

$$\frac{d\rho}{dt} = -\frac{i}{\hbar}[H_C, \rho] + \gamma\left(\mathcal{A}\rho\mathcal{A}^\dagger - \frac{1}{2}\{\mathcal{A}^\dagger\mathcal{A}, \rho\}\right)$$

**Key insight**: The "branches" are **not ontological duplicates**. They are **unravelings** (different measurement representations of the same density matrix \(\rho\)).

### 8.1.3 Path-Integral Form

**Theorem 8.1.3** (Feynman Path Integral for Stochastic Dynamics): The probability of a specific trajectory is:

$$P[\psi] \propto \exp\left(-\frac{S[\psi]}{\hbar}\right)$$

Where the **action** is:

$$S[\psi] = \int_0^T \mathcal{L}_t[\psi(t)] dt$$

With Lagrangian:

$$\mathcal{L}_t = \langle \psi | \left(i\frac{d}{dt} + H_C + \frac{\gamma}{2}\mathcal{A}^\dagger\mathcal{A}\right) | \psi \rangle$$

**Consequence**: The multicomputation ensemble is a **weighted path ensemble** with weight function \(\exp(-S/\hbar)\).

## 8.2 Distinguishing Branches: Observable Separation

### 8.2.1 Branch Definition Operationally

**Problem**: How do we define "distinct branches" quantitatively?

**Answer**: Branches are **distinguishable** if trajectories can be separated by measurement of an observable \(\mathcal{B}\):

$$\mathcal{B} = \sum_j b_j |b_j\rangle\langle b_j|$$

**Definition 8.2.1** (Branch Distinguishability): Two trajectories \(\psi_i, \psi_k\) are in **distinct branches** if:

$$D_{ik} = |\langle \psi_i | \mathcal{B} | \psi_k \rangle| < D_{threshold} \approx 0.1$$

**Concrete example**: 
- \(\mathcal{B} = |0\rangle\langle 0| - |1\rangle\langle 1|\) (Pauli-Z)
- If \(\psi_i \approx |0\rangle\) and \(\psi_k \approx |1\rangle\), then \(D_{ik} \approx 0\) → distinct branches

### 8.2.2 Branch Stability Criterion

**Definition 8.2.2** (Stable Branch): A trajectory family \(\{\psi_i\}\) forms a stable branch if:

$$\tau_{coherence} \gg \tau_{measurement}$$

Where:
- \(\tau_{coherence}\): Dephasing time (maintains superposition)
- \(\tau_{measurement}\): Measurement back-action timescale

**Consequence**: Branches remain distinguishable for at least one measurement cycle.

### 8.2.3 Number of Branches

**Theorem 8.2.3** (Maximum Branch Count): For an \(n\)-qubit system with measurement projecting onto basis \(\{|b_j\rangle\}\):

$$n_M^{max} = 2^n$$

(Dimension of Hilbert space)

**Typical case** (product measurements):
$$n_M \approx \lfloor \sqrt{2^n} \rfloor \quad \text{(stabilizable branches)}$$

For 10 qubits: \(n_M^{max} = 1024\), stabilizable \(n_M \approx 45\).

**Interpretation**: This is **not** Many-Worlds (infinite branches). This is measurement-limited classical ensemble.

## 8.3 Multicomputation Branching During Optimization

### 8.3.1 Branch Dynamics in Gradient Descent

**Theorem 8.3.1** (Branching Under Non-Selective Measurement): As optimization proceeds with **non-selective continuous measurement** (weak monitoring without post-selection):

1. Initially: Single coherent trajectory
2. After \(\tau_{deco}\): Trajectory **splits** into \(n_M\) distinguishable branches
3. Each branch: Follows different gradient path (due to measurement perturbation)
4. Ensemble average: Recovers Lindblad master equation

**Mathematical description**:
$$|\psi(t)\rangle = \sum_{j=1}^{n_M(t)} a_j(t) |\psi_j(t)\rangle$$

With time-dependent amplitude:
$$|a_j(t)|^2 = \text{Prob(branch j at time t)}$$

**Evolution**:
$$\frac{d|a_j|^2}{dt} = -\frac{1}{\tau_j}|a_j|^2 + \text{regeneration}$$

Above \(\mathcal{T}_c\): regeneration \(>\) decay → stable multicomputation.

### 8.3.2 Branching Entropy

**Definition 8.3.2** (Stability Metric): Define multicomputation entropy:

$$S_M(t) = -\sum_{j=1}^{n_M} |a_j|^2 \ln|a_j|^2$$

**Interpretation**:
- \(S_M = 0\): Single branch (coherent)
- \(S_M = \ln(n_M)\): Maximally mixed (all branches equally probable)

**Behavior across transition**:

| Temperature | Regime | Entropy | Branches |
|-------------|--------|---------|----------|
| T < Tc | Below | S_M ≈ 0 | ~1 (unstable) |
| T = Tc | Critical | S_M ≈ 0.5 ln(n_M) | ~√n_M |
| T > Tc | Above | S_M ≈ 0.9 ln(n_M) | ~n_M (stable) |

**Experimental signature**: Measure information content of classical shadow of quantum state.

## 8.4 Connection to Weak-Value Formalism

### 8.4.1 Weak Values as Branch Indicators

**Definition 8.4.1** (Weak Value): Under weak measurement of observable \(\mathcal{O}\):

$$\mathcal{O}_w = \frac{\langle f | \mathcal{O} | i \rangle}{\langle f | i \rangle}$$

Where \(|i\rangle\), \(|f\rangle\) are initial and final states.

**Property**: Weak values can be **outside** the spectrum of \(\mathcal{O}\) for post-selected ensembles.

**Connection to branches**: Each branch \(\psi_j\) has a distinct weak-value trajectory:

$$\mathcal{O}_{w,j}(t) = \frac{\langle f_j | \mathcal{O} | \psi_j(t) \rangle}{\langle f_j | \psi_j(t) \rangle}$$

**Measurement protocol**: 
1. Perform weak measurement of \(\mathcal{O}\)
2. Posterior-select on final state \(|f_j\rangle\)
3. Extract weak value \(\mathcal{O}_{w,j}\)
4. Each distinct \(\mathcal{O}_{w,j}\) indicates different branch
